Challenges of Winter Pruning

Current Issues

  • Winter pruning is the most labor-intensive vineyard operation
  • Requires specialized workforce
  • Estimated 30-40% labor shortage compared to demand
  • Incorrect pruning leads to production decreases

Impact on Vineyards

  • Risk of incomplete seasonal pruning
  • Increased operational costs
  • Time constraints due to limited pruning windows
  • Disease vulnerability from improper cuts

"Many companies risk not completing seasonal pruning due to lack of pruners, estimated at 30-40% less than needed."

- Confagricoltura Emilia Romagna

State of the Art

Mechanization in Viticulture

In the wine sector, most operations have been mechanized. For winter pruning, the cordon spur system involves an initial pre-pruning phase that's already automated, but the second phase remains manual.

This second phase represents an important opportunity for automation to make viticulture more efficient and technologically advanced.

Autonomous & Efficient Pruning

A robot capable of performing cordon spur pruning without specialized labor would be the ideal solution. While this technology exists experimentally, it's not yet commercially available.

Our Robot: potAI

Recognizes the Vine

Using YOLO deep learning model, potAI detects key vine components (trunk, shoot, bud...) with high accuracy.

Precision Cutting

Based on element position and count, the robot determines optimal cutting positions using a 6-DOF industrial robotic arm.

Autonomous Navigation

Using precision GPS and pre-mapping of vineyard layout, the robot navigates between fields and positions itself parallel to vines.
